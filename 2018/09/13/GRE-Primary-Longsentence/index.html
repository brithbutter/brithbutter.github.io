Primary Grammar for Long Sentence in GRE Reading

parallel structure

  • this structure is the basic structure in long sentences. Normally we use “and” and “or” to connect the words or sub sentences.
  • other than Parataxis kind of parallel structure, adversative relation is also a kind of parallel structure in long sentences.

    ex: “not … but …”

    not with their synthesis rate, but rather with the equally variable rates at …

  • special adverb structure can also lead a parallel structure

    “from … to … to …”

    ex: from a sovereign people to compatriots with newly arriving settlers to a conquered people.

Ellipsis (omittance) element

  • When we are using “and” and “or” to state several sentences, whose subjects or other duplicated elements, we can omit these elements. By the way, “,” and “;” are also compatible for this usage.

    We are hard pressed on every side, but (we are) not crushed; (we are) perplexed, but (we are) not in despair; (we are) persecuted, but (we are) not abandoned; (we are) struck down, but (we are) not destroyed.

    For instance, some of the novels appear to be structurally diffusive. Is this a defect, or are the authors working out of, or (are the authors) trying to forge, a different kind of aesthetic?

    In this case, the phrase of subject and verb is omitted.

Adverbial clause

  • Clause are major components of long sentences. Therefore, I will list several situations of clause as notes.

    Because

    The complete sentences led by “Because” work as Adverbial clauses. This kind of clauses is called conditional adverbial clause, which illustrate the preconditions by complete sentence.

    make up: “in view of”, “given”

    Conditional Adverb

    Conditional adverb will describe the specific condition of this sentence.

    “If” will lead a clause as a conditional Adverb

    “Only when” can also lead a conditional Adverb. However, it will lead a complete sentence as a clause.

    Examples

    the examples led by words “like”, “such as” and “as” are also recognized as an adverb in a sentence. However, they are not looks like a complete sentence. Therefore, they are actually adverb phrases.

    So that

    the purposes led by word “so that” are also recognized as an adverb in a sentence. In case of “so that”, “so” can be omitted.

    Adj + Adv

    the phrases started with adjective and adverb can be recognized as a adverb in a sentence.

    ex: capable of, distinct from

    Time adverb

    • the adverb subordinate led by “when”
    • the clause led by “during”

    Contrast Subordinate Clause

    This kind of clauses is attempted to make the main idea of long sentence more convincing.

    • led by “Although” or “Though”

Subjunctive mood

  • Subject mood is used when people presume some preconditions for their statement. Normally the verbs in these sentences should degrade their tense and be arranged into a flip structure.

    Nevertheless, researchers of the Pleistocene epoch have developed all sorts of more or less fanciful model schemes of how they would have arranged the Ice Age had they been in charge of events.

    In this sentence, “had they been in charge of events” is using Subjunctive mood. Therefore, the auxiliary verb, “had” is moved ahead of the subject. Besides, the “if” is omitted.

subject inversion

Sometimes, we may move adverb ahead to rearrange the order of the components in a sentence, in case misunderstanding the point of the sentence caused by a subject consist of a long phrase.

Moreover, to complete the sentence structure, we may use “it” as a formal subject to suggest a coming complete subject.

Accordingly, it requires a major act of will to think of price-fixing (the determination of prices by the seller) as both “normal” and having a valuable economic function.

in this case, “it” refers to “to think…”. Therefore, sentences like “it + be + adj + to xxx” can also be recognized as subject inversion.

Apposition Clause

Once we are using a word need to explain with a complete sentence, we’d better use “that” to connect the noun and the sentence.

ex: sb. holds a arguments that xxx
